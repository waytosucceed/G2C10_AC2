3 letter consonants, Consonant Blends, and Multisyllabic words


Grammar Topic

Three-letter consonants are groups of three consonants that work together to make a unique sound.
Consonant blends are combinations of two or more consonants that blend to create a new sound.
Since we do hear two sounds, they are considered blends.
Consonant blends can be found at the beginning, middle, or end of words.

Word meaning:

Ventured:
To go somewhere or do something that involves risk or uncertainty.
Example: Despite the warnings, she ventured into the dark forest.

Familiar:
Well-known from long or close association.
Example: After travelling abroad for a year, it was comforting to return to the familiar streets of her hometown.

Stumbled: To trip or lose one's balance in walking or running.
Example: He stumbled over a stone and nearly fell while hiking.
